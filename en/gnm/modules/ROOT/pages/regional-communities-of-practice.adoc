= Developing Capacity within Regional Communities of Practice

== Context

Since 2008, GBIF has supported six regional networks around the world to encourage and strengthen regional collaboration and information exchange among Participants. 
These regional sub-committees are explained in the https://assets.ctfassets.net/uo17ejk9rkwj/73Shop9ogovEILrXFGLW6R/933d0e904fce33c762bd0d3b0480f7a1/Terms_of_Reference_for_the_Nodes_Committee_-_September_2017.pdf[Terms of Reference for the Nodes Committee^], and on the https://www.gbif.org/nodes[GBIF nodes page^].

A key theme across the regional sub-committees is the coordination of outreach actions to encourage broader engagement in GBIF at all levels, particularly as formal Participants in the network. 
The GBIF Governing Board commissioned an https://www.gbif.org/news/5WZThcL928vmPnSvrGhZfE/report-reveals-return-on-investments-in-gbif[economic valuation of the GBIF network^] to demonstrate the return on investments in GBIF. 
The study by Deloitte Access Economics published this year supports Participants in outlining GBIFâ€™s value proposition and the benefits of being in the network. 
We hope that regions can reflect on how this study can help to support outreach efforts. 

During the Global Nodes Meeting, the elected regional representatives will organize regional breakout sessions, to discuss strengthening regional engagement and plans for regional nodes meetings in 2024.
